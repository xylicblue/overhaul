This Privacy Policy describes how ByteStrike ("we," "us," or "our") collects, uses, and shares your information when you visit byte-strike.com or use our software, including community plugins for the Obsidian note-taking application.
1. Our Commitment to Your Privacy
We follow "Privacy by Design" principles, ensuring that data protection is integrated into our software from the start. As a developer in the Obsidian ecosystem, we prioritize private and local-first usage. We do not have access to your local Obsidian vaults or notes unless specifically authorized by you for a feature that requires cloud processing.
2. Information We Collect
Information You Provide: This includes account registration details (email, username), billing information for paid products, and correspondence from support requests.
Automatically Collected Data: When you visit our site, our servers log standard data provided by your web browser, such as your IP address, browser type, and the pages you visit.
Cookies: We use cookies to maintain your session identity and remember your preferences. You can disable cookies in your browser settings, though some site functionality may be affected.
Plugin Usage Data: Our plugins do not collect client-side telemetry without your explicit opt-in. If enabled, we may collect anonymized usage metrics or error logs to improve performance.
3. Legal Bases for Processing
We only process your personal data when we have a valid legal basis:
Consent: Where you have given clear consent for a specific purpose (e.g., newsletter signup).
Contract: When processing is necessary to fulfill a contract with you (e.g., providing a software license).
Legitimate Interest: To protect our services from fraud and to improve our products, provided these interests are not overridden by your rights.
4. Sharing and International Transfers
We do not sell your personal data. We may share data with service providers who assist our operations, such as cloud hosting (AWS/Google) and payment processors. Data may be stored or processed in countries outside of your residence; we ensure these transfers comply with legal standards like the GDPR.
5. Your Rights
Depending on your location (e.g., EU or California), you have the following rights regarding your data:
The right to access and receive a copy of your data.
The right to rectify (correct) inaccurate information.
The right to erasure ("right to be forgotten").
The right to data portability.
The right to withdraw consent at any time.
6. Data Security and Retention
We use industry-standard security measures, including AES-256 encryption and TLS, to protect your data. We retain your information only as long as necessary for the purposes outlined in this policy or to comply with legal obligations.
7. Children's Privacy
Our services are not directed to individuals under the age of 16 (or 18 in certain regions). We do not knowingly collect personal data from children.
8. Changes to This Policy
We may update this policy periodically. We will notify you of any significant changes by posting a notice on our website or contacting you via email.
